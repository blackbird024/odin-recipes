Lasagna

El mejor plato italiano...

Lasagna (or lasagne) is a quintessential Italian comfort food consisting of wide, flat pasta sheets layered with rich fillings like meat ragù, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese. While globally synonymous with the Bolognese style (meat sauce and béchamel), its variations across Italy range from the Neapolitan tradition featuring meatballs and hard-boiled eggs to modern vegetarian and seafood interpretations.

Ingredients

For the ragù (meat sauce)

  • 500g ground beef (or mixed)
  • 1 onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 400g crushed tomatoes
  • Olive oil, salt, pepper

For the béchamel

  • 50g butter
  • 50g flour
  • 500ml milk
  • Salt, pepper and nutmeg

For the assembly

  • Lasagna pasta sheets
  • 200g mozzarella cheese
  • 100g grated parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Choose the Sauces: Simmer your meat sauce (ragù) for at least an hour for deep flavor. For the béchamel, whisk flour into melted butter (roux) and gradually add milk until thickened.
  2. Your Pasta: Use "oven-ready" no-boil sheets for speed, or traditional sheets that require a quick blanch in boiling water.
  3. Assemble: Start with a smear of sauce on the bottom of the pan to prevent sticking. Layer pasta, meat sauce, béchamel (or ricotta), and a generous sprinkle of Parmesan or mozzarella. Repeat for at least 3–5 layers.
  4. Bake: Cover with foil and bake at 350°F (180°C) for about 30 minutes, then uncover for the final 10–15 minutes to brown the cheese.
  5. Rest: Always let your lasagna rest for at least 15 minutes before cutting; this ensures the layers set and stay together.
